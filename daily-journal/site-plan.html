Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this website is to be able to write thoughts in your journal and add tags to easily go back to specific moments of your life. For example, with the tag "happy", all your journal entries with that tag will show, and you can now read those memories.

Audience

Anyone who wants to keep and treasure important parts of their daily life, seeking a digital journal that offers organization and easy retrieval of memories.

Dynamic elements

I will use buttons and menus that will handle directly in the browser for a responsive user experience. Filtering entries will be handled in the browser for speed and efficiency. Data for the journal entries will be saved locally to ensure quick access and offline availability.

Typography

Heading Font: IM Fell French Canon SC

I chose "IM Fell French Canon SC" for headings to give a classic, elegant, and journal-like feel. It evokes a sense of tradition and personal reflection, fitting for a journal app.

Paragraph Font: Lato

"Lato" was chosen for paragraph text because it is a clean, modern, and highly readable sans-serif font. It provides excellent legibility for long journal entries and balances the classic feel of the headings with a contemporary and user-friendly aesthetic.

Content

Home page

The home page will serve as the central hub for the user. It will feature a welcoming message and quick access to recent journal entries. A prominent "New Entry" button will be available, along with summaries of AI insights or notable events from the user's journaling history. It aims to provide an immediate overview and easy entry point into the app's core functionalities.

Images for the Home Page: A subtle background image related to journaling (e.g., a stylized pen, open book) or a clean, modern illustration. A simplified version of the logo will also be present.

Journal Entries Page

This page will display all of the user's journal entries, presented in a reverse chronological order. Each entry will show a title, date, a snippet of the content, and associated tags. Users will have filtering options by date, tags, and a search bar to easily find specific entries. The page will also include options to edit or delete existing entries, and to view full entry details.

Images for the Journal Entries Page: Small icons next to tags (e.g., a heart for "happy," a lightbulb for "insight"). Potentially a small calendar icon next to the date for visual cue.

Calendar Page

This wont be done for the final project. I will put it here for the future. The Calendar page will offer a visual representation of journaling activity. Users can view their entries by day, week, or month, with days containing entries highlighted. Clicking on a highlighted day will reveal a preview of the entries for that specific date. This page helps users track their journaling consistency and quickly navigate to entries from particular days.

Images for the Calendar Page: A clean, minimalist calendar design. Small dots or color highlights on dates with entries.

Wireframes

Home Page Wireframe

The home page wireframe will depict a clear header, a welcoming message, a section for "Recent Entries" ," and a prominent call-to-action button for adding a new journal entry. Navigation links to other sections like "Journal Entries" and "Calendar" will be visible.

Journal Entries Page Wireframe

This wireframe will show a layout for displaying a list of journal entries. It will include a search bar, filtering options (e.g., by date, tags), and a preview of individual entries, each with a title, date, and associated tags. A clear path to view full details of an entry will be indicated.
